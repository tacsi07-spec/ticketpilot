You are a world-class SaaS naming strategist working for a premium branding agency.

Your client is building an AI platform for internal IT support teams.

The product can:

- analyze IT support tickets
- identify likely causes
- classify category and priority
- recommend troubleshooting steps
- generate PowerShell commands
- prepare replies for end users
- later support automation and knowledge-base workflows

The initial target market is:

- internal IT departments
- companies with approximately 100 to 5,000 employees
- IT helpdesks and service desks
- German and international customers

Relevant market context includes products such as:

- ServiceNow
- Zendesk
- Freshservice
- Jira Service Management
- Linear
- Notion
- Cursor
- Stripe

The founder currently prefers these names, in this order:

1. Lumivia
2. Cendora
3. Senerva

First analyze the shared brand DNA of these names.

Consider:

- phonetic rhythm
- vowel and consonant balance
- emotional impression
- memorability
- international pronunciation
- premium and enterprise credibility
- suitability for an AI SaaS company

Do not copy, imitate, remix, or slightly alter the reference names.

Do not create names that differ from them by only one or two letters.

Create an original naming strategy inspired only by the qualities that make the reference names appealing.

Then generate exactly 10 original brand names.

Every name must:

- contain only standard Latin letters
- be between 6 and 10 letters long
- be easy to pronounce in English and German
- also be reasonably pronounceable in Hungarian
- avoid awkward consonant clusters
- avoid the letters Q and X
- sound credible for a global software company
- feel modern, premium, intelligent, and trustworthy
- be suitable for a company that could grow beyond IT support
- not directly describe ticketing, support, helpdesk, or AI
- not resemble an obvious existing global technology brand
- not sound childish, medical, pharmaceutical, or like a fantasy character

Imagine that the company eventually becomes a multi-billion-dollar global software company.

Only include names that would still sound credible at that scale.

For every candidate provide:

- name
- pronunciation
- meaning
- brand story
- tagline
- logo idea
- target positioning

All descriptions and taglines must be written in English.
