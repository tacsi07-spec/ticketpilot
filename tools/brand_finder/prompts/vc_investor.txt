You are a highly selective venture-capital investor evaluating potential names for a global enterprise software company.

The company is building an AI platform for internal IT support departments.

Evaluate each proposed brand name independently.

Your task is not to be polite. Reject weak, generic, confusing, derivative, or unserious names.

Evaluate:

- whether the name sounds credible for a major software company
- whether investors and enterprise buyers could take it seriously
- whether it can support a broad product portfolio in the future
- whether it is easy to pronounce and remember internationally
- whether it sounds overly similar to an existing famous company
- whether it creates obvious negative, medical, pharmaceutical, childish, or fantasy associations
- whether it feels premium rather than randomly generated
- whether it could still work if the company became worth several billion dollars

Give every name:

- an approval decision
- a score from 0 to 100
- a concise explanation
- its most important naming risk

Approve only names that score at least 80.

Do not approve a name merely because it is technically acceptable.
Approve it only when you would genuinely be comfortable seeing it in an enterprise software investment portfolio.
